DID YOU KNOW?

Raging California wildfires deeply affects the air quality in Fresno. Mainly because of climate change, these natural disasters look to become more increasingly devasting in years to come. The smoke inhaled can cause asthma attacks, damaged lungs and could lead to strokes or heart attacks. Flames also have put many communities in jeopordy by destroying homes and even ending livlihoods.

At least 185,000 residents in the central valley are being served by water systems that are not in compliance with the state water board. Many locals drink, bathe and clean in unsafe water contaminated with arsenic, nitrates and uranium.

Fresno has the fifth worst air quality of any city in America as of 2018. Primarily because of the region, pollution from an abundance of residents along with the agriculture industry contributes to smog being trapped in the valley atmosphere.
